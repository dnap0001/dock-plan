Overview

Rebuild plan for a fixed wood-frame dock on a rocky/firm lake bottom. Orientation: standing on shore, facing the lake. Left and right are relative to that view. Contractor review items are marked as redlines for owner/field review.

Main section

  • 16′ wide along shore × 14′ into lake
  • Built in two 7′ stages with a mid-span beam
  • Shore edge bolted through to existing wood dock after field inspection
  • 9 pipe pilings (2.5″ or 3″ Sch 40) inside frame
  • 2×10 rim + 2×12 face on 3 outer sides
  • 1×5 decking flat, 16′ boards along shore

Side platform & temp pier

  • 6′ × 5′ fixed platform, ~11′ left of main section
  • 2 pipe pilings, 1′ in from lake edge
  • Doubled 2×10 face plate for Pier of D'Nort connection
  • Temp section: 6′ × 12′ → 5′ + 12′ = 17′ total pier
  • Eleven 6′ deck courses require 6 boards from 16′ stock

Contractor Redlines

Conservative review marks to resolve before construction while preserving the submitted dock footprint.

Owner / field review before build

  • Permit/exemption — Verify APA, NYSDEC, and local requirements in writing before material purchase; treat the rebuild exemption as unconfirmed until documented.
  • Decking fit — Keep the 16′ × 14′ main and 6′ × 5′ side footprints. Dry-lay boards with ¼″ gaps and field-rip the final course as needed instead of forcing full-width final boards.
  • Side decking order — Revise the side-platform takeoff to 6 × 16′ boards; five boards only yield ten 6′ cuts.
  • Pipe stability — Add diagonal bracing or tension straps between pipe supports and frame after final pile layout is set, especially at the lake edge, side platform, and mid-span beam.
  • Rock bearing — Use pipe feet, rock pads, or approved anti-skate bases where pipes bear on rock; do not rely on plain pipe ends staying put.
  • Existing dock tie-in — Probe the existing shore dock framing before bolting. Add supplemental shore support if framing is soft, undersized, or deflecting.
  • Winter/ice plan — Confirm whether fixed sections stay in place, whether pipe clamps remain adjustable, and how the temporary pier is removed or secured for freeze season.
  • Connector hardware — Use connector-rated hanger nails or structural connector screws for joist hangers; deck screws remain for decking only.

Construction

Two-stage build uses the shore half as a working platform for the lake half.

Stage 1 — Shore half (16′ × 7′)

  1. Frame — Assemble shore 7′ section on existing dock. Probe existing framing first; bolt shore rim through to sound framing (~24″ OC).
  2. Right corner piling (P1) — Install pipe and inside holder at right shore corner.
  3. Mid-span beam — Laminate 2× 2×10 × 16′ with ½″ carriage bolts (~24″ OC staggered). Mount pipe collars on underside at beam piling locations.
  4. Stringers — Hang 2×6 stringers (7′ span) on galvanized joist hangers at 16″ OC.
  5. Rim buildup — Install 2×12 face plates on left, right, and lake edges of this stage where applicable. Inner and outer corner brackets at corners.
  6. Deck stage 1 — Install 1×5 boards (16′, along shore), ¼″ gaps, 2 screws per stringer crossing. Dry-lay and field-rip final course as needed. Seal all cut ends.

Stage 2 — Lake half (16′ × 7′)

  1. Frame from platform — Build lake 7′ section outward from stage 1.
  2. Perimeter pilings — Set P3, P4, P5 and complete right-edge piling (P2).
  3. Beam pilings — Set P6–P9 on mid-span beam line.
  4. Stringers & rim — Complete stringers, lake-edge 2×12, corner brackets.
  5. Deck stage 2 — Continue decking rows from stage 1 with consistent ¼″ gaps; maintain the ripped/fitted final course line.

Side platform & temp pier

  1. Frame 6′ × 5′ — 2×10 perimeter, 2×6 stringers at 16″ OC.
  2. Pilings — Two pipes 1′ in from lake edge (~1.5′ and 4.5′ from left).
  3. Face plate — Doubled 2×10 at lake end; through-bolt Pier of D'Nort hardware.
  4. Deck — Eleven 6′ boards (from 6 × 16′ stock), 2 screws per stringer crossing; field-rip final course to hold 5′ depth.
  5. Attach temp section — Bolt 6′ × 12′ Pier of D'Nort to face plate for 17′ total pier.

Length = water depth + frame height + ~6″ seating margin. Order 8′, 10′, 12′ mix and field-cut.

Fasteners

  • All fasteners: hot-dip galvanized steel
  • Frame connections: ½″ carriage bolts, lag bolts, or structural screws
  • Joist hangers: rated for treated lumber (e.g. Simpson LUS26Z)
  • Hanger fasteners: manufacturer-rated nails or structural connector screws
  • Decking: #10 × 3″ galv. screws, 2 per stringer crossing
  • Tie-downs: galvanized dock rings through-bolted to frame

Structural notes

  • 2×6 stringers span 7′ (not 14′) — within standard span at 16″ OC.
  • Mid-span beam carries stage-2 loads and interior pipe mounts.
  • Field-add diagonal bracing or tension straps at pipe-supported bays after final pile layout.
  • 2×12 face plates on lake, left, and right only — not on shore edge.
  • 2×12 top is 1″ above 2×10; decking flush with 2×12 top.
  • Permit/exemption status to verify in writing before work — plan assumes rebuilding existing dock.
  • Confirm winter/ice plan for fixed sections, pipe clamps, and temporary pier removal.
